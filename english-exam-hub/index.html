Reading Skills (22 Marks)

Passage Strategy

  • Scan Questions First: Know what you're looking for before reading.
  • Highlight Keywords: Mark names, dates, and core concepts.
  • MCQ Hack: Use elimination method for tricky options.

Creative Writing (18 Marks)

FORMAT (4 Marks)
[NAME OF ISSUING ORGANIZATION]
NOTICE
[DATE OF ISSUE]
[HEADING/TITLE]

[Body of the notice: What, When, Where, Who, How]

[SIGNATURE]
[NAME]
[DESIGNATION]
  • Keep it under 50 words.
  • Draw a box around it! (Compulsory)
INVITATION & REPLY (4 Marks)

Formal: Third person, no signature, include RSVP.

Informal: First person, like a letter.

JOB APPLICATION (5 Marks)

Includes Cover Letter + Bio-data (Resume).

Bio-data: Name, Parentage, DOB, Address, Qualifications, Experience, References.

MUST-KNOW LITERARY DEVICES
  • Simile: Comparison using 'as' or 'like' (e.g., "pale as a late winter's moon").
  • Metaphor: Direct comparison (e.g., "The paper-seeming boy").
  • Alliteration: Repetition of consonant sounds (e.g., "finger-fluttering").
  • Hyperbole: Exaggeration (e.g., "a hundred years to praise").
  • Irony: Contrast between expectation and reality (e.g., The Tiger King's death).

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting the BOX in Notice/Invitation.
  • Writing more than 150 words in Letters (Marks cut!).
  • Mixing up 1st and 3rd person in Invitations.

Level Up: Vocabulary & Characters 🚀

Power Vocabulary

  • Poignant: Use for The Last Lesson (sadness).
  • Resilience: Use for Deep Water (strength).
  • Stigma: Use for Lost Spring (social shame).
  • Escapism: Use for The Third Level.
  • Indomitable: Use for Gandhi in Indigo.

Character Starters

M. Hamel: "A true patriot who transformed from a stern teacher to a broken man..."

Saheb-e-Alam: "A victim of systemic poverty whose name ironically means 'Lord of the Universe'..."

Douglas: "A survivor who proved that 'all we have to fear is fear itself'..."

🎭 Tone & Mood Booster

Common Tones to look for in Extract Questions:

  • Ironical: When the title or situation is opposite (e.g., Saheb-e-Alam's name).
  • Nostalgic: Remembering the past (e.g., My Mother at 66).
  • Didactic: Meant to teach a lesson (e.g., Keeping Quiet).
  • Critical/Satirical: Attacking a social evil (e.g., Lost Spring).

RTC (Extract) Cracker 🕵️‍♀️

Strategy for Prose Extracts

  • Context is King: Identify the speaker and the situation immediately.
  • Read between the lines: Look for irony or sarcasm (especially in The Tiger King or Poets and Pancakes).

Strategy for Poetry Extracts

  • Identify Devices: Every stanza will have at least one literary device.
  • Theme Link: Always connect the lines back to the poem's central message.
